How Much Do Property Managers Charge Per Month? Hiring a good property manager is more of an asset than an expense for successful real estate investors. The best property management companies help you keep your rental property occupied year after year, increase your return on investment (ROI), and free up your valuable time. 1. Percentage of Monthly Rent When a vacant property is leased to a new tenant, management companies typically charge a fixed fee or a fee equal to the anticipated monthly rent. Vacant properties may require additional management work, such as weekly inspections for potential break-ins or the presence of trespassers. 2.Fixed Property Management Fee Some property managers charge a flat fee rather than a percentage of the monthly rent. Usually, the fixed property management charges depend on the property type, square footage, and property management services provided. The fixed property management charges will vary by market. While a flat fee structure may seem a good deal at first glance, management companies that collect a fixed fee may not be as motivated to maximize your property's rental income. 3. Sublet/Guaranteed Rent Model The Property Management Company will ideally ask you to sign a sublease agreement with them where they will acquire the power to sub-let your property to preferred tenants. Here you will lose control over your property. You would get lower rent than the market value, as clearly the management company would need to earn a profit, even after taking a hit of the vacancy loss. It is difficult to ensure monthly payments when your property is vacant, hence the chances of default are very high, as we have seen with almost all companies offering this model, during the pandemic. 4. Enhanced Comfort and Enjoyment Smart home technology is also making our lives more comfortable and enjoyable. With the help of smart home entertainment systems, you can enjoy your favourite music and movies in high-quality audio and video. In addition, some smart home devices can also be programmed to create a personalised atmosphere, like by adjusting the lighting and temperature to match your mood or the occasion. 5. Improved Well-being Perhaps the most exciting aspect of smart home technology is its potential to improve your overall well-being. It's the best option for working parents with little infants or people with elderly parents. The smart cameras installed in the home can help you keep an eye on your children or your elderly parents and monitor their activities throughout the day. It's especially helpful for the elderly as it can perform a wide range of functions. For example, notifying them when it's time to take their medicine, turning off taps and lights in case they forget, alerting homeowners in emergency situations, etc.
